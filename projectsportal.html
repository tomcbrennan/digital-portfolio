Project Sportal

Mobile Application

A comprehensive sporting application, serving to cohesively motivate students to utilise Curtin's modern sporting facilities & remain active around campus.

The

Problem

Remaining fit and active has always been a challenging task for people whose time is heavily occupied by work and study. Curtin University is home to a wealth of sporting facilities at the Bentley campus. There is however a serious lack of students who are both motivated & willing to utilise these facilities and exercise when on campus.

Project

Goals

Motivate students & staff to exercise frequently on campus

Promote continuity of exercise throughout weekly campus visits

Raise awareness of sporting facility access for both students and staff

Key Features

Your solution to exercising on campus.

Project Sportal provides users with a hub of listed sporting activities from other users at campus. The dashboard screen of the app invites users to explore the live mapping feature, with visualised sport listings from other students on campus. Listings are displayed at the location that the host has specified, with an icon for the respective sporting activity.

Join a listed sporting activity.

Students and staff are able to view listed sports on the live map feed and search filter, depending on their exercise preference. Once a student has found a suitable event and time slot, they are able to join the activity. This activity is then listed into their upcoming activities panel. When the activity has been concluded by the host, participants receive reward icons and progress towards their weekly fitness goals.

List your own sporting activity.

Curtin University contains a plethora of sporting facilities that are available for use by students. Project Sportal allows students to list their own sporting activities during their free time in study breaks or inbetween classes. The aim of this process is to make exercising a more social and inclusive activity that encourages those who do not particularly enjoy exercisng. The ability to list your own activity reduces the constraints that other forced fitness activities may implement.

View your weekly exercise log.

The user's weekly exercise activity is logged within the menu, displaying their total exercise time with their own activity listings, as well as how far off their weekly goal they are. This design feature, along with a rankings system, was implement to motivate users to continue pushing for their fitness goals each week.

What did I learn?

Project Sportal presented me with my first concrete opportunity to improve my ability in collating user research and produce an overall solution that adhered to this research. More specifically, being a problem primarily targeted at students on campus, I was able to easily obtain the wants, needs and perspectives of students that aided my proposed solution immensely. Furthermore, throughout the prototyping process, I was able to experiment with using programs that were previously so foreign to me. Initial prototypes were constructed using Figma, with the final solution and micro-animations made possible with Adobe XD. With software like these being heavily used throughout modern design industry, I was pleased to be able to develop these vital skills during the development of Project Sportal.
